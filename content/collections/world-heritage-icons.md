# World Heritage Icons

**Status:** RC14 flagship collection draft  
**Architecture boundary:** Implementation content only; Architecture v1.0 remains frozen under ADR-011.

## Introduction

World Heritage Icons is a flagship draft collection for globally recognized places selected for public-memory learning. It is designed for validation-only editorial, educational, and product exploration. The collection does not create canonical records, new agents, governance changes, or production commerce commitments.

## Educational narrative

This collection helps learners connect individual assets to wider systems of geography, culture, biodiversity, public memory, and stewardship. It is written for museum-shop discovery, classroom prompts, family learning, and future rights-cleared product testing.

## Key assets

- Machu Picchu
- Great Pyramid of Giza
- The Great Wall
- Taj Mahal
- Stonehenge

## Key facts

- World Heritage status signals global significance but does not replace local stewardship.
- Iconic sites can introduce geography, archaeology, conservation, and cultural continuity.
- Rights-cleared imagery is required before production use.
