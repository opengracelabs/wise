## Introduction

Conservation has its icons — and its emergencies. This collection gathers the species and ecosystems whose survival has become a measure of our own choices: the panda that became the symbol of a movement, the rhino hunted for myth, the reef bleaching under a warming sea.

## Educational narrative

Endangerment is not a single story but many: poaching drives the rhino and the pangolin; habitat loss closes in on the orangutan and the gorilla; a warming climate threatens the polar bear's ice and the Great Barrier Reef's coral. Presenting them together turns scattered headlines into a coherent appeal — and, paired with the right partners, into support for the work of protection.

## Key assets

- **Snow Leopard (Panthera uncia)** (Central Asia) — Elusive 'ghost of the mountains' of the high Himalaya.
- **Mountain Gorilla (Gorilla beringei)** (Africa) — Critically endangered great ape of the Virunga volcanoes.
- **Giant Panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca)** (China) — Bamboo-eating bear, the global symbol of wildlife conservation.
- **Polar Bear (Ursus maritimus)** (Arctic) — Apex predator of the sea ice and a symbol of climate change.
- **Black Rhinoceros (Diceros bicornis)** (Sub-Saharan Africa) — Critically endangered browser, a flagship of anti-poaching efforts.
- **Orangutan (Pongo)** (Indonesia) — Critically endangered great ape of Borneo and Sumatra.
- **Pangolin (Manis)** (Africa) — Scaled mammal, the world's most trafficked animal.
- **Great Barrier Reef** (Australia) — World's largest coral reef system, visible from space and rich in marine life.
