# African Kingdoms

**Status:** RC14 flagship collection draft  
**Architecture boundary:** Implementation content only; Architecture v1.0 remains frozen under ADR-011.

## Introduction

African Kingdoms is a flagship draft collection for African cultural heritage beyond wildlife-only representation. It is designed for validation-only editorial, educational, and product exploration. The collection does not create canonical records, new agents, governance changes, or production commerce commitments.

## Educational narrative

This collection helps learners connect individual assets to wider systems of geography, culture, biodiversity, public memory, and stewardship. It is written for museum-shop discovery, classroom prompts, family learning, and future rights-cleared product testing.

## Key assets

- Great Zimbabwe
- Lalibela Rock-Hewn Churches
- Timbuktu Manuscript Heritage
- Aksum Obelisks
- Swahili Coast Stone Towns

## Key facts

- African heritage includes cities, manuscripts, architecture, trade networks, rock art, and living traditions.
- This collection corrects overreliance on wildlife as the primary African portfolio story.
- Some materials require partner clearance and sensitivity review.
