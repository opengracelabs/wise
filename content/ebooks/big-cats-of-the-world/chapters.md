# Big Cats of the World - Draft Chapters

## Chapter 1: What makes a big cat?

Big cats capture attention because they combine power, beauty, movement, and mystery. In this ebook, they are treated not as isolated animals but as living indicators of habitat health, conservation pressure, and human imagination.

## Chapter 2: Lions and the social life of predators

The African lion introduces grassland ecosystems, pride behavior, and the challenge of conserving large predators near human communities. Learners compare social hunting, habitat range, and the symbolism of lions across cultures.

## Chapter 3: Tigers and forests under pressure

Tigers help readers understand forests, camouflage, river systems, and conservation corridors. The chapter connects tiger visibility in popular culture to the less visible work of habitat protection.

## Chapter 4: Snow leopards and mountain ecosystems

Snow leopards lead readers into high mountain environments where climate, prey species, and local communities shape conservation outcomes. The chapter emphasizes adaptation and landscape connectivity.

## Chapter 5: Jaguars and rainforest corridors

Jaguars introduce the Americas, rainforest ecology, and the importance of corridors. Readers learn why a species can be a symbol of both biodiversity and Indigenous cultural meaning.

## Chapter 6: Cheetahs and speed in open landscapes

Cheetahs reveal how anatomy, speed, and open habitat are linked. The chapter also shows how specialization can make a species vulnerable when landscapes change.

## Chapter 7: Big cats in culture and public imagination

This chapter compares how big cats appear in stories, symbols, flags, museum collections, and conservation campaigns. It asks learners to separate admiration from responsible stewardship.

## Chapter 8: Conservation, coexistence, and future habitats

The final narrative chapter connects protected areas, community stewardship, climate pressure, and species recovery. It ends with a question: what does it mean to share a planet with apex predators?

## Chapter 9: Classroom activities and visual prompts

Activities include map reading, habitat comparison, predator-prey web drawing, conservation poster critique, and species profile cards.

## Chapter 10: Product and collection guide

The ebook closes by connecting educational content to validation-only products: posters, canvas prints, puzzles, calendars, card sets, and the Big Cats collection.
