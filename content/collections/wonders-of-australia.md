## Introduction

Australia is a continent of superlatives: the oldest living culture, the largest reef, animals found nowhere else, and a monolith at its red heart. This collection gathers the natural and cultural wonders that define it.

## Key assets

- **Sydney Opera House** (Australia) — Expressionist sail-roofed icon of modern architecture.
- **Uluru-Kata Tjuta** (Australia) — Sacred monolith of the Anangu in the Australian outback.
- **Great Barrier Reef** (Australia) — World's largest coral reef system, visible from space and rich in marine life.
- **Twelve Apostles** (Australia) — Limestone sea stacks along the Great Ocean Road.
- **Red Kangaroo (Osphranter rufus)** (Australia) — Largest marsupial and emblem of Australia.
- **Koala (Phascolarctos cinereus)** (Australia) — Iconic Australian marsupial of the eucalyptus forest.
- **Kakadu Rock Art** (Australia) — Aboriginal rock paintings spanning tens of thousands of years.
- **Platypus (Ornithorhynchus anatinus)** (Australia) — Egg-laying monotreme, one of evolution's marvels.

## Educational narrative

Australia's wonders span deep time and the present day — from 65,000 years of Aboriginal rock art at Kakadu and the sacred monolith of Uluru, to the egg-laying platypus and the modern sails of the Sydney Opera House. Read together they introduce a place where the ancient and the new, the natural and the built, sit side by side.
