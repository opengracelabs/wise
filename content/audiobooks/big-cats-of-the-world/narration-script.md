## Opening

Welcome to Nature & Culture. This is Big Cats of the World, a short listening guide to the animals people recognize instantly, and the habitats, histories, and responsibilities that make those animals more than symbols.

Pause for two seconds.

When we hear the word lion, many of us imagine a mane, a pride, a savanna, or a story told long before we were born. When we hear tiger, we may imagine stripes moving through a forest. These images are powerful. But public memory asks for more than recognition. It asks for context.

## Chapter 1 - The cats we recognize

A big cat can become an emblem before it becomes an understood species. This chapter asks listeners to slow down. What is the animal? Where does it live? What does it need? What do people project onto it? Recognition opens the door, but evidence helps us walk through it.

## Chapter 2 - Habitat holds the story

No big cat exists outside a place. A lion story is also a grassland story. A tiger story is also a forest story. A snow leopard story is also a mountain story. A jaguar story is also a river, wetland, and forest story. When we listen for habitat, we hear a fuller record.

## Chapter 3 - Reading images responsibly

A photograph can inspire wonder, but it cannot answer every question. Is the animal wild or captive? Is the species named correctly? Does the caption include place and source? What is outside the frame? Nature & Culture treats images as records with questions attached.
