## 1. Purpose of This Document

This document establishes the mission, constitutional relationship, and governing intent for the Open Grace and Nature & Culture institutions. It is the authoritative statement of *why* the system exists and *how* governance and public mission are separated for long-term institutional durability.

All architectural, physical, and implementation decisions documented in [03-canonical-architecture.md](03-canonical-architecture.md), [05-physical-architecture.md](05-physical-architecture.md), and [06-build-roadmap.md](06-build-roadmap.md) derive from the principles stated here.

---

## 2. Nature & Culture — Public Mission

**Nature & Culture** is the public-facing institution. It is the name, voice, and experience through which humanity encounters the permanent digital memory of heritage, nature, and culture.

### Mission Statement

> Build the permanent digital memory of humanity's heritage, nature, and culture, freely accessible to every person, in every language, for all future generations.

This statement is canonical and must not be altered without constitutional amendment under Open Grace governance (see [08-decision-record.md](08-decision-record.md), ADR-001).

### Public Mandate

Nature & Culture exists to:

1. **Preserve** — Acquire, steward, and perpetually maintain digital representations of humanity's natural and cultural heritage.
2. **Connect** — Link objects, places, species, languages, traditions, and knowledge across time, geography, and discipline.
3. **Publish** — Make preserved knowledge freely accessible through search, exploration, education, research, and public products.
4. **Include** — Serve every person regardless of language, ability, connectivity, or geography.
5. **Endure** — Operate on a 100-year architectural horizon with generational continuity of mission and trust.

Nature & Culture does not own constitutional authority. It operates under the governance framework established by Open Grace.

---

## 3. Open Grace — Constitutional Governance Layer

**Open Grace** is the constitutional governance layer. It is not a public brand and not a user-facing product. It is the durable institutional framework that ensures Nature & Culture remains faithful to its mission across leadership changes, funding cycles, technological shifts, and political pressures.

### Constitutional Functions

Open Grace provides:

| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Mission protection** | Immutable charter enforcement for Nature & Culture's public mission |
| **Architectural authority** | Canonical architecture ownership via the Open Grace Architecture Office |
| **Stewardship standards** | Preservation, provenance, and access policies binding on all systems |
| **Succession governance** | Rules for leadership transition, institutional continuity, and amendment |
| **Openness guarantees** | Constitutional commitment to free access, open formats, and public interest |
| **Inter-institutional covenant** | Framework for partnerships with UNESCO-aligned bodies, archives, museums, and research institutions |

### Separation of Powers

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    OPEN GRACE                               │
│         Constitutional Governance Layer                     │
│  Charter · Standards · Architecture · Succession            │
└─────────────────────────┬───────────────────────────────────┘
                          │ governs
                          ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│               NATURE & CULTURE                              │
│            Public-Facing Institution                        │
│  Experience · Publishing · Education · Community · Products │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Open Grace sets *what must be true*. Nature & Culture delivers *what the public experiences*. This separation is intentional and permanent (see [08-decision-record.md](08-decision-record.md), ADR-002).

## 4. The 100-Year Horizon

The institutions are designed for a **100-year canonical architecture horizon** — long enough to outlive any single technology generation, short enough to plan concretely.

### Generational Design Principles

1. **Format independence** — Content survives format obsolescence through normalization, emulation, and migration (see [07-reference-standards.md](07-reference-standards.md)).
2. **Governance over technology** — Constitutional rules precede implementation choices.
3. **Reference-model grounding** — Design draws from proven institutions (see [02-reference-models.md](02-reference-models.md)).
4. **Progressive build** — The founder build order in [06-build-roadmap.md](06-build-roadmap.md) sequences capability from preservation inward to public experience outward.
5. **Geographic and institutional redundancy** — No single point of failure in geography, vendor, or governance (see [05-physical-architecture.md](05-physical-architecture.md)).

### What "100-Year" Means Operationally

| Timeframe | Focus |
|-----------|-------|
| **Years 1–10** | Foundational platforms: discovery, ingestion, preservation, knowledge graph, search |
| **Years 10–30** | Global scale: translation fabric, research fabric, education, community, observatories |
| **Years 30–100** | Institutional permanence: succession, format migration, geographic expansion, covenant renewal |

## 5. Scope of the Canonical Memory

The permanent digital memory encompasses:

- **Heritage** — Monuments, artifacts, sites, intangible traditions, oral histories, archaeological records
- **Nature** — Species, ecosystems, biodiversity records, environmental observations, geological and paleontological data
- **Culture** — Languages, arts, literature, music, customs, belief systems, and lived cultural expression

All three domains are unified under a single knowledge architecture (see [03-canonical-architecture.md](03-canonical-architecture.md)) rather than siloed collections.

---

## 6. Non-Negotiable Commitments

The following commitments are constitutional and bind all systems, partnerships, and public experiences:

1. **Free access** — No paywall on canonical public memory.
2. **Every language** — Translation and localization are first-class architectural capabilities, not afterthoughts.
3. **Open formats** — Preservation formats and APIs favor open, documented standards.
4. **Provenance integrity** — Every object maintains auditable chain of custody and attribution.
5. **Public interest primacy** — Commercial products may exist; they must not compromise the public mission.
6. **Amendment discipline** — Changes to mission, architecture, or governance require documented decision records.

## 8. Amendment Process

Amendments to this mission charter require:

1. A formal proposal to the Open Grace Architecture Office
2. An architecture decision record in [08-decision-record.md](08-decision-record.md)
3. Explicit notation of what is being changed and why
4. A minimum review period appropriate to the scope of change

The Nature & Culture mission statement (Section 2) requires the highest amendment threshold: unanimous constitutional consent under Open Grace governance.
