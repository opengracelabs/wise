## Narration

Nature & Culture is building a permanent digital memory of humanity's heritage, nature, and culture. That mission begins with records: places, species, artworks, maps, and stories that help people understand the world they inherit.

Today we look at big cats, habitats, and source-aware image reading. We will ask three practical questions: What are we seeing? What source supports the record? What can the subject teach when we connect visual attention with context?

This episode does not treat images as decoration. It treats them as entry points. A good public record should invite curiosity, then answer that curiosity with source notes, careful language, and a path toward deeper learning.
