# African Kingdoms

> The cities, courts and empires of Africa's past.

## Introduction

Africa's history is a history of kingdoms — of gold and salt, of bronze and stone, of scholarship and faith. This collection restores to the portfolio a continent too often left out of the global story, from the stone city of Great Zimbabwe to the libraries of Timbuktu.

## Key assets

- **Great Zimbabwe** (Zimbabwe) — Largest medieval stone city in sub-Saharan Africa, capital of a gold-trading kingdom.
- **Benin Bronzes** (Nigeria) — Masterworks of West African brass casting from the Kingdom of Benin.
- **Timbuktu** (Mali) — Medieval centre of Islamic scholarship and trans-Saharan trade.
- **Pyramids of Meroe** (Sudan) — Steep Nubian royal pyramids of the Kingdom of Kush.
- **Rock-Hewn Churches of Lalibela** (Ethiopia) — Eleven monolithic churches carved from rock, a living pilgrimage site.
- **Nok Terracotta** (Nigeria) — Among the oldest sculptural traditions of sub-Saharan Africa.

## Educational narrative

These are civilizations of the first rank: the brass casters of Benin, whose plaques rival any Renaissance relief; the Nubian pharaohs whose steep pyramids crowd the desert at Meroë; the Ethiopian masons who carved churches downward into living rock at Lalibela. Presenting them together is both an educational correction and a celebration — African achievement on its own terms.
