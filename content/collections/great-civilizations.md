# Great Civilizations

**Status:** RC14 flagship collection draft  
**Architecture boundary:** Implementation content only; Architecture v1.0 remains frozen under ADR-011.

## Introduction

Great Civilizations is a flagship draft collection for heritage places that anchor long human timelines. It is designed for validation-only editorial, educational, and product exploration. The collection does not create canonical records, new agents, governance changes, or production commerce commitments.

## Educational narrative

This collection helps learners connect individual assets to wider systems of geography, culture, biodiversity, public memory, and stewardship. It is written for museum-shop discovery, classroom prompts, family learning, and future rights-cleared product testing.

## Key assets

- Great Pyramid of Giza
- The Great Wall
- Taj Mahal
- Angkor Wat
- Acropolis of Athens

## Key facts

- Civilizations can be studied through architecture, engineering, belief, trade, and memory.
- Monuments remain powerful because they carry both local history and global recognition.
- Comparative learning helps students see continuity and difference across world regions.
