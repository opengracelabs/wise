## Introduction

For a thousand years, goods, faiths and ideas flowed along the Silk Road — and at its great oasis cities, they pooled into some of the most beautiful architecture on earth. This collection gathers the tiled madrasas, minarets and walled towns of that in-between world.

## Educational narrative

The Silk Road was less a road than a web, and its cities were its knots: Samarkand's turquoise domes, Bukhara's holy towers, Khiva's walls, the cliff niches of Bamiyan where Buddhism travelled west. They tell a story of exchange rather than conquest — of a Central Asia that was, for centuries, the centre of the world.
