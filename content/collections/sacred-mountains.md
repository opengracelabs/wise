# Sacred Mountains

**Status:** RC14 flagship collection draft  
**Architecture boundary:** Implementation content only; Architecture v1.0 remains frozen under ADR-011.

## Introduction

Sacred Mountains is a flagship draft collection for mountains as cultural, ecological, and spiritual landscapes. It is designed for validation-only editorial, educational, and product exploration. The collection does not create canonical records, new agents, governance changes, or production commerce commitments.

## Educational narrative

This collection helps learners connect individual assets to wider systems of geography, culture, biodiversity, public memory, and stewardship. It is written for museum-shop discovery, classroom prompts, family learning, and future rights-cleared product testing.

## Key assets

- Machu Picchu
- Uluru-Kata Tjuta National Park
- Kilimanjaro National Park
- Pamir Mountains
- Mount Fuji

## Key facts

- Mountain landscapes often combine biodiversity, water systems, pilgrimage, and cultural identity.
- Sacred-site content requires cultural sensitivity and local context.
- Climate change makes mountain ecology and glaciers important learning topics.
