## Introduction

No animals hold the human imagination like the big cats. Worshipped in Egypt, emblazoned on heraldry, and stalking the myths of every continent, they are at once icons of power and barometers of the wild places that remain. This collection gathers the world's great cats as a single portrait of strength, beauty and fragility.

## Educational narrative

The big cats are an *umbrella* group: protect the lion's savanna, the tiger's forest or the snow leopard's high valleys, and a whole web of life is protected with them. Their ranges trace the planet's last great wildernesses, and their decline tracks our own footprint. Telling their story together — across Africa, Asia and the Americas — lets readers see both the shared anatomy of the perfect predator and the very different pressures each species faces, from poaching to habitat loss to human conflict.

## Key assets

- **Lion (Panthera leo)** (Africa) — The 'king of beasts', a universal emblem of strength across human cultures.
- **Tiger (Panthera tigris)** (Asia) — Largest living cat and an endangered icon of Asian wilderness.
- **Leopard (Panthera pardus)** (Sub-Saharan Africa) — Widest-ranging big cat, elusive and powerful.
- **Snow Leopard (Panthera uncia)** (Central Asia) — Elusive 'ghost of the mountains' of the high Himalaya.
- **Jaguar (Panthera onca)** (South America) — Largest cat of the Americas and apex predator of the Amazon.
- **Cheetah (Acinonyx jubatus)** (Africa) — Fastest land animal, sprinting across the open savanna.
