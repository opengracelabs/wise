## Introduction

Long before they were climbed, mountains were worshipped. They are where earth reaches for sky, where gods are said to dwell, and where pilgrims still walk. This collection gathers the world's sacred summits — natural wonders that are also spiritual ones.

## Educational narrative

From Fuji's near-perfect cone to the red monolith of Uluru, sacred mountains anchor the cosmologies of the peoples around them. Some are climbed in devotion, others left deliberately unclimbed out of respect. Together they show how landscape and belief shape one another — and why protecting a peak can mean protecting a culture.
